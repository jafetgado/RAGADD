Patients report varying effects on libido while taking stimulant medications like Adderall or Vyvanse. Some individuals experience a decrease in libido, while others mention a significant increase in sexual desire. These changes in libido are noted alongside other positive and negative effects of the medications on focus, mood, and weight.
ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals report feeling emotionally volatile and overjoyed by little things while on amphetamines like Adderall or Vyvanse, with the potential for extreme emotional reactions to minor triggers. On the other hand, methylphenidates like Concerta can lead to feeling emotionally flat, hollow, and even becoming virtually emotionless, impacting relationships and causing depressive symptoms. It's important to monitor mood changes and consider additional medications to balance emotions when necessary.
The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. Patients may experience this crash as the medication wears off, leading to discomfort and a significant impact on their daily functioning. Some individuals may try managing this by adjusting their dosage or considering alternative medications to address the emotional side effects.
Patients report that immediate-release ADHD formulations like Adderall immediate release can lead to mood swings and irritability when wearing off, while extended-release formulations like Vyvanse extended release can also cause mood swings and irritability. However, some individuals find that extended-release formulations provide a smoother and more sustained effect throughout the day without the highs and lows associated with immediate-release medications.
The most difficult side effects to manage with Strattera and Atomoxetine include insomnia, nausea, body aches, headache, irritability, urinary issues, sexual side effects, heavy menstrual cycles, sweating, chills, dry mouth, and changes in mood. Strategies commonly used to manage these side effects include adjusting the dosage, using sleep aids like Trazadone for insomnia, accepting trade-offs for improved productivity, and hoping for side effects to diminish over time.
Daytrana, a patch-based medication for ADD, has been reported to provide effective and long-lasting medication compared to pills for some individuals. Users have noted benefits such as improved concentration and behavior. However, some have experienced side effects like skin irritation, decreased appetite, and difficulty sleeping when the patch is worn for an extended period. Despite these challenges, many users find that the benefits of Daytrana outweigh the negatives in managing symptoms of ADD.
Long-term use of ADHD medications like Adderall and Methylphenidate can lead to concerns such as stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, depression, anxiety, emotional flatness, high blood pressure, low libido, binge eating, and mood swings. Users also worry about the impact on heart and liver health, as well as the potential for dependency and withdrawal symptoms.
Patients report varying experiences with dosage adjustments to maintain effectiveness of medications like Adderall and its variants. Some individuals find that increasing the dosage leads to improved effectiveness initially, but may also experience negative effects such as dizziness, stumbling over words, headaches, and concerns about long-term health implications. Others mention a plateau in effectiveness over time, leading to a need for dosage adjustments to address diminishing benefits, with some expressing concerns about dependency and side effects like increased sweating, higher pulse rate, higher blood pressure, lower quality of sleep, and muscle tension.
