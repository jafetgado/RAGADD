Patients report that Concerta is perceived as better than Adderall for some individuals, providing a more subtle and focused effect compared to Adderall's more intense impact on focus. Some individuals find that Concerta helps with focus without significant side effects, while Adderall may lead to more noticeable mood changes and appetite suppression.
Patients report positive personality changes during treatment with Guanfacine or Intuniv, such as improved communication, emotional control, compliance, focus, and reduced impulsivity. Some individuals experience increased agreeableness, reduced emotional reactivity, and better memory recall. However, there are also reports of initial side effects like crankiness, sleepiness, and increased tics when doses are missed.
Patients report that stimulant medications like Adderall and Vyvanse can negatively impact libido or sexual desire. Some individuals experienced a decrease in sex drive while taking these medications, leading to concerns about potential libido issues with prolonged use. Switching to other medications like Dexedrine was mentioned as a hopeful solution to improve sexual function.
Patients report that Methylphenidates can lead to feeling emotionless, hollow, and socially avoidant, while Amphetamines like Adderall and Vyvanse can make individuals extremely emotional and volatile. Vyvanse specifically is noted for causing extreme joy over small things but also being prone to triggering strong reactions to minor annoyances. Some individuals may require additional medications to balance mood when taking Vyvanse.
The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation, typically occurring in the mid-afternoon. Some individuals may experience this crash as uncomfortable and seek to manage it by potentially increasing their medication dosage to prolong its effects.
Immediate-release ADHD formulations like Adderall can lead to mood swings and irritability when wearing off, while extended-release formulations like Vyvanse aim to provide a steady effect throughout the day without the need for multiple doses. Some individuals prefer the longevity and consistent impact of extended-release formulations to avoid highs and lows associated with immediate-release medications.
The most difficult side effects to manage with Strattera and Atomoxetine include insomnia, nausea, body aches, headache, irritability, burning urination, suicidal thoughts, sweating, chills, dry mouth, headaches, and menstrual irregularities. Strategies commonly used to manage these side effects include prescribing sleep aids like Trazadone for insomnia and monitoring for improvement over time, though some individuals may find the side effects intolerable and discontinue the medication.
Daytrana, a patch-based medication for ADD, is reported to provide long-lasting medication compared to pills for some individuals. Users mention minimal skin irritation as a side effect. However, there are concerns about potential dependency on the patch for focus, leading to difficulty focusing without it and feelings of being unmotivated or grumpy.
Long-term use of ADHD medications like Adderall and Methylphenidate can lead to potential side effects such as stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, weight loss, depression, anxiety, and emotional flatness. Monitoring by healthcare providers is crucial due to uncertainties regarding the full extent of long-term side effects.
Patients report needing to increase their dosage of Adderall XR to maintain effectiveness, with one individual mentioning going from 15 mg to 20 mg and another from 20 mg to 30 mg. However, increasing the dosage can lead to concerns about potential negative effects, such as dizziness, speech difficulties, daily headaches, and the need for additional medication to manage symptoms.
