Patients report varying effects on libido while taking stimulant medications like Adderall and Vyvanse. Some individuals mention experiencing a decrease in libido, while others note a significant increase in sexual desire. These changes in libido are often accompanied by other effects such as improved focus, weight loss, and mood alterations.
ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals report feeling emotionally volatile, experiencing extreme joy or irritability with amphetamines like Adderall or Vyvanse. Others mention feeling emotionally blunted or becoming more emotionally stable with medications like Methylphenidate. It is important to monitor mood changes and consider the impact of these medications on emotional well-being.
The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often characterized by irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation as the medication wears off. Patients may also experience feelings of depression, emptiness, anger, and irritability during this period. To manage the crash, some individuals consider adjusting their dosage, timing of medication intake, avoiding stimulants, maintaining a consistent diet, exercising in the morning, and having medication-free days.
Patients report that immediate-release formulations provide quick onset of action but may lead to crashes and mood swings when the medication wears off. On the other hand, extended-release formulations offer a smoother and more consistent effect throughout the day, reducing the need for multiple doses and minimizing highs and lows. However, some individuals find extended-release medications less effective in controlling symptoms or experience shorter duration of action than expected.

Daytrana, a patch-based medication, has been reported to provide steady and controlled doses, improving focus and behavior in individuals with ADHD. Users have noted benefits such as improved school performance, reduced impulsivity, and better sleep. However, some experienced side effects like skin irritation, decreased appetite, and difficulty falling asleep if the patch is worn for an extended period. Overall, the positive outcomes of Daytrana seem to outweigh the negatives for many users.
Long-term use of ADHD medications such as Adderall, Methylphenidate, and Amphetamine/dextroamphetamine can lead to various side effects. These may include stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, depression, anxiety, mood swings, weight loss, emotional flatness, insomnia, liver and kidney concerns, high blood pressure, low libido, binge eating, and potential dependency issues. Users have reported concerns about the impact on overall health, relationships, and well-being over time.
Patients often report that over time, the effectiveness of their ADHD medication diminishes, leading to the need for dosage increases. However, increasing the dosage can result in negative consequences such as developing tolerance, experiencing side effects like headaches, dizziness, stumbling over words, and increased reliance on the medication. Patients express concerns about the long-term effects on health and the need for continual dosage adjustments to maintain effectiveness.
