Patients report that after starting Guanfacine or Intuniv, they observe improvements in communication, emotional expression, focus, behavior, and impulse control. Some individuals experience increased agreeableness, reduced emotional reactivity, improved self-esteem, and better compliance with tasks. However, a few patients may initially experience side effects such as tiredness, irritability, or changes in sleep patterns, which can vary in severity and duration.
Patients report varying effects on libido while taking stimulant medications like Adderall and Vyvanse. Some individuals mention a decrease in libido, while others note a significant increase in sexual desire. These changes in libido are experienced alongside other effects such as improved focus, weight loss, and mood alterations.
ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood. Some individuals report experiencing extreme emotions, increased volatility, and heightened pleasure with amphetamines like Adderall and Vyvanse. On the other hand, methylphenidates such as Concerta are described as stabilizing mood with little personality change, although some individuals may experience emotional blunting or rage during the comedown period. It is important to monitor emotional responses and consider additional medications to balance mood when necessary.
The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often characterized by irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation as the medication wears off. Patients may also experience feelings of depression, emptiness, anger, and emotional volatility during this period. To manage the crash, some individuals may consider adjusting their dosage, timing of medication intake, avoiding stimulants, maintaining a consistent diet and exercise routine, and incorporating medication-free days.
Patients report that immediate-release ADHD formulations provide quick onset of effects but may lead to crashes and mood swings when the medication wears off. On the other hand, extended-release formulations offer a smoother and longer-lasting effect, helping to maintain a more consistent level of focus throughout the day without the highs and lows associated with immediate-release medications.
The most difficult side effects to manage with Strattera and Atomoxetine include insomnia, nausea, body aches, irritability, burning urination, suicidal thoughts, heavy menstrual cycles, dry mouth, headaches, difficulty urinating, frothy ejaculations, constipation, anger, and extreme tiredness. Strategies commonly used to manage these side effects include adjusting dosage, using sleep aids like Trazodone, accepting trade-offs for improved productivity, and considering medication changes with the guidance of a psychiatrist.
Daytrana, a patch-based medication for ADHD, has been reported to provide steady and controlled doses, improving focus, behavior, and school performance. Users mention benefits such as avoiding impulse control issues, maintaining normalcy, and achieving academic success. However, some experienced skin irritation, decreased appetite, and sleep difficulties, while others noted dependency concerns and challenges with patch removal.
Long-term use of ADHD medications like Adderall, Methylphenidate, and Amphetamine/dextroamphetamine can lead to various side effects. These include concerns about stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, depression, anxiety, mood swings, emotional flatness, weight loss, blurred vision, stomach aches, insomnia, liver and kidney issues, dependency, tolerance, dizziness, stumbling over words, headaches, and potential impact on relationships and emotional well-being. Users also report concerns about potential long-term impacts on health, including high blood pressure, low libido, binge eating, and difficulty quitting the medication.
Patients often report needing to increase the dosage of medications like Adderall or Amphetamine/dextroamphetamine over time to maintain effectiveness. However, increasing dosages can lead to negative effects such as dizziness, stumbling over words, headaches, increased sweating, higher pulse rate, higher blood pressure, lower quality of sleep, and muscle tension. Some patients also express concerns about potential long-term health effects and dependency on these medications.
