ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals on medications like Vyvanse may experience extreme emotional states, ranging from overjoyed to easily upset, while others on Methylphenidate may feel emotionally flat or become consumed with rage during the medication's comedown period. Different medications can impact individuals differently, with some reporting mood stabilization and improved emotional control, while others may experience increased aggression or emotional volatility.

The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. Patients may also experience increased fatigue and decreased energy levels. To manage this crash, some individuals consider adjusting their dosage to prolong the medication's effects, while others may seek additional medications to help balance their mood.

Patients report that immediate-release formulations like Concerta provide quick onset of action but can lead to abrupt crashes once the medication wears off, causing irritability and anger. In contrast, extended-release formulations like Vyvanse and Adderall XR offer a smoother and more sustained release of medication, reducing mood swings and irritability. However, some individuals may still experience challenges with extended-release medications, such as shorter-than-expected duration of effect or delayed onset of action.
