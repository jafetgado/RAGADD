Patients report varying effects on libido while taking stimulant medications like Adderall and Vyvanse. Some individuals mention experiencing a decrease in libido, while others note a significant increase in sexual desire. These changes in libido are often accompanied by other effects such as improved focus, weight loss, and mood alterations.

ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals report feeling emotionally volatile, experiencing extreme joy or irritability with amphetamines like Adderall or Vyvanse. Others mention feeling emotionally blunted or becoming more emotionally stable with medications like Methylphenidate. It is important to monitor mood changes and consider the need for additional medications to balance mood when using stimulants for ADHD.

The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. Patients may also experience increased irritability and agitation as the medication wears off. To manage this, some patients consider adjusting their dosage to prolong the effects and delay the onset of the crash.

Patients report that immediate-release formulations provide quick onset of action but may lead to crashes and mood swings when the medication wears off. On the other hand, extended-release formulations offer a smoother and more consistent effect throughout the day, reducing the need for multiple doses and minimizing highs and lows. However, some individuals find extended-release medications less effective in controlling symptoms or experience issues with duration of action.

The most difficult side effects to manage with Strattera and Atomoxetine include insomnia, nausea, body aches, irritability, burning urination, suicidal thoughts, menstrual irregularities, dry mouth, headaches, difficulty urinating, frothy ejaculations, constipation, anger, and extreme tiredness. Patients often try strategies such as adjusting dosage, using sleep aids like Trazadone, accepting trade-offs for improved productivity, and seeking consultation with psychiatrists to address these challenging side effects.

Daytrana, a patch-based medication, has been reported to provide steady and controlled doses, improving focus and behavior in individuals with ADHD. Users have noted benefits such as improved school performance, reduced impulsivity, and better sleep. However, some experienced side effects like skin irritation, decreased appetite, and difficulty falling asleep if the patch is worn for an extended period.

Long-term use of ADHD medications like Adderall, Methylphenidate, and Amphetamine/dextroamphetamine can lead to concerns such as stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, depression, anxiety, mood swings, emotional flatness, weight loss, blurred vision, stomach aches, insomnia, liver and kidney issues, dependency, and potential impact on relationships and emotional well-being. Users also report negative effects on sleep, muscle spasms, trouble with sleep, and potential cognitive decline after prolonged use.

Patients often report that over time, the effectiveness of their ADHD medication, such as Adderall or Adderall XR, may diminish, leading to the need for dosage increases. However, increasing dosages can come with negative consequences, including side effects like dizziness, stumbling over words, headaches, increased sweating, higher pulse rate, higher blood pressure, lower quality of sleep, and muscle tension. Some individuals also express concerns about potential long-term health effects and dependency on the medication.
