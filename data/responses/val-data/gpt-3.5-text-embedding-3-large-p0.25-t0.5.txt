ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals report feeling emotionally volatile, experiencing extreme joy or irritability with amphetamines like Adderall or Vyvanse. Others mention feeling emotionally blunted or becoming more emotionally stable with methylphenidates such as Concerta or Ritalin. It is important to monitor mood changes and consider additional medications to balance emotions when necessary.

The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. Patients may also experience increased irritability and agitation as the medication wears off. To manage this, some patients consider adjusting their dosage to prolong the effects and delay the onset of the crash.

Immediate-release formulations like Concerta and Adderall provide quick onset of action but may lead to crashes and mood swings when the medication wears off. Extended-release formulations like Vyvanse and Daytrana offer a smoother and longer-lasting effect, but some patients report issues with duration and effectiveness, leading to the need for additional doses or booster medications. Individual responses vary based on personal experiences and preferences.
