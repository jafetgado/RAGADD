ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. It is important to monitor emotional changes and consider the need for additional medications to balance mood when using stimulants for ADHD.

The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. To manage this, some patients consider adjusting their dosage to extend the medication's duration or seek additional medications to help balance their mood.

Patients report that immediate-release formulations provide quick onset of action but may lead to crashes and mood swings when the medication wears off. However, some individuals find extended-release medications less effective in controlling symptoms or experience shorter duration of action than expected.
