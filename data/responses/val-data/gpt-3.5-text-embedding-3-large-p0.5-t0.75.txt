Patients report varying effects on libido while taking stimulant medications like Adderall and Vyvanse. Some individuals mention experiencing a decrease in libido, while others note a significant increase in sexual desire. These changes in libido are often accompanied by other effects such as improved focus, weight loss, and mood alterations.

ADHD medications can have varying effects on emotional regulation and mood variability. Some individuals report feeling emotionally volatile, experiencing extreme joy or heightened emotions, while others mention feeling emotionally flat or even becoming emotionless. Additionally, some individuals may experience mood swings, irritability, or increased emotional sensitivity as side effects of ADHD medications.

The emotional "crash" from ADHD medications is often described as involving irritability, anxiety, moodiness, sleepiness, and agitation. Patients may experience this crash as the medication wears off, leading to discomfort and agitation. Some individuals may try managing this by adjusting their dosage or timing of medication intake.

Patients report that immediate-release formulations like Concerta provide quick onset of action but can lead to abrupt crashes when the medication wears off, causing irritability and anger. On the other hand, extended-release formulations like Vyvanse and Adderall XR offer a smoother and more steady release of medication, reducing mood swings and irritability. However, some patients find that extended-release medications may not last as long as expected, requiring additional doses throughout the day.

The most difficult side effects to manage with Strattera and Atomoxetine include insomnia, nausea, body aches, irritability, burning urination, suicidal thoughts, menstrual irregularities, difficulty urinating, frothy ejaculations, constipation, dry mouth, anger, and extreme tiredness. Patients have reported using strategies such as taking sleep aids like Trazadone, managing diet, accepting trade-offs for improved productivity, and considering medication changes with their healthcare providers to address these challenging side effects.

Daytrana, a patch-based medication, has been reported to provide steady and controlled doses, improving focus and behavior in individuals with ADHD. Users have noted benefits such as improved school performance, reduced impulsivity, and better management of day-to-day routines. However, some users experienced side effects like skin irritation, decreased appetite, and difficulty falling asleep if the patch is worn for an extended period.

Long-term use of ADHD medications like Adderall, amphetamine/dextroamphetamine, and methylphenidate can lead to concerns such as stunted growth, loss of appetite, headaches, palpitations, depression, anxiety, mood swings, weight loss, emotional flatness, blurred vision, stomach aches, insomnia, liver and kidney issues, and potential dependency. Users also report negative impacts on relationships, emotional well-being, and physical health, with some experiencing severe side effects like high blood pressure, low libido, binge eating, and emotional numbness.
